How It Works

The Commission Process

Every piece begins with a conversation. We work closely with each client to ensure the final table is exactly right for their space and vision.

Inquiry

Submit your vision — dimensions, species preference, base style, and intended use. We respond within 48 hours.

Slab Selection

We present available slabs matching your criteria. For dining tables, we source bookend pairs to echo each other across the river.

Design & Build

A detailed sketch is approved before work begins. Build time is typically 8–14 weeks depending on complexity.

Delivery

White-glove delivery and installation in the Denver metro area. Nationwide crating and freight shipping available.

The Materials

Wood,
Water,
Steel

Every slab begins its story long before it enters the studio. Wayward Grain sources timber exclusively from small family logging operations in the Rocky Mountain region and Pacific Northwest — operations that harvest selectively and with intention. Logs are aged after milling to allow moisture to equalize and natural figure to deepen, producing the dramatic color variations that define each piece.

The glass rivers and lake inlays are cast in-house. Each pour is mixed, colored, and finished by hand — no two rivers run the same course.

Steel bases are fabricated locally in Denver. We offer matte black powder coat, brushed stainless, raw steel with oil finish, and genuine hammered bronze.

Common Questions

Before You Reach Out

Do you ship nationwide?

Yes. White-glove delivery and installation is available throughout the Denver metro area. For clients outside Colorado, all pieces are professionally crated and shipped via fine-art freight carriers — fully insured, door to door.

Where do your slabs come from?

Slabs are sourced from regional sawyers, estate timber, and a small network of sustainable suppliers across the Rocky Mountain West and upper Midwest. Every slab is hand-selected by Jason for figure, character, and structural integrity.

What types of furniture do you make?

Wayward Grain specializes in live-edge and bookend dining tables, river tables, coffee tables, console and entryway tables, and custom desks. Each piece is a one-of-a-kind commission — if you have something specific in mind, ask.

How long does a commission take?

Current lead time is 10–14 weeks from deposit to delivery. Timelines vary by complexity, slab availability, and finish selection. Jason will give you a project-specific estimate during your consultation.

What are the payment terms?

A 50% deposit is required to secure your place in the build queue. The remaining balance is due prior to delivery or pickup. We accept check, bank transfer, and all major credit cards.

What does a piece typically cost?

Dining tables start at $6,500 and coffee tables from $4,500, depending on slab selection, dimensions, and base design. River tables and highly figured slabs may be priced higher. Every quote is custom.
